## What Candidates Commonly Say

### Positives
- **Compensation & perks:** Bay Area leaders (Google, Meta, NVIDIA, Apple) consistently rank high on total comp, equity, and benefits.
- **Structured interviews:** Larger companies use standardized rubrics, which candidates find fairer and more predictable.
- **Strong recruiters:** Many reviews praise responsive, communicative recruiting teams at LinkedIn, Salesforce, and Adobe.
- **Learning & brand value:** Time at these firms is widely seen as a strong résumé and skill multiplier.

### Common Frustrations
- **Long timelines:** Multi-round loops plus committee/hiring-committee review can stretch decisions out (notably Google).
- **High difficulty bar:** Algorithm-heavy or system-design rounds (Meta, Stripe, NVIDIA) are cited as demanding.
- **Ghosting / silence:** Even top firms get dinged for slow or no follow-up after onsites, especially in down-cycles.
- **Repetitive loops:** 4–6 similar technical rounds can feel exhausting and redundant.

---

## Interview Process — Typical Stages
1. **Recruiter screen** — role fit, background, logistics, comp expectations.
2. **Technical / phone screen** — 1–2 rounds (coding, domain, or take-home).
3. **Onsite / virtual loop** — 4–6 rounds: coding, system design, behavioral, sometimes domain-specific.
4. **Team match & hiring committee** — review and leveling (common at Google/Meta).
5. **Offer & negotiation** — comp, equity, start date.

---

## Tips for Candidates
- **Practice fundamentals:** Data structures, algorithms, and system design remain the core technical bar.
- **Prepare behavioral stories:** Values-based rounds (Amazon-style or Airbnb/Salesforce "values" interviews) reward concrete examples.
- **Ask about timelines up front:** Set expectations to avoid the "silence" frustration.
- **Use leveling/comp data:** Sites like Levels.fyi help benchmark offers before negotiating.
- **Cross-check culture:** Read recent (last 6–12 months) reviews — culture and hiring pace shift quickly with market cycles.
